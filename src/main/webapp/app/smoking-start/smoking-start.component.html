You are good at self control?



INSTRUCTIONS :


No Smoking for A Day.


Challenge yourself to go without smoking for an entire day. Twenty-four hours smoke-free can significantly benefit your heart rate and blood pressure, beginning to reduce the risks associated with smoking. Prepare strategies in advance for dealing with cravings, such as keeping your hands busy, staying hydrated, or using nicotine replacement therapies if necessary. Reflect on how you feel physically and mentally without cigarettes for a day, using these observations as motivation to extend smoke-free periods.

You are almost there.



INSTRUCTIONS :


No Smoking For 2 Days.


Extend your no-smoking goal to 48 hours. After two days without cigarettes, your body will continue to clear carbon monoxide from your system, and your lungs will begin to clear out mucus and other smoking debris. Notice any improvements in your senses, such as taste and smell, starting to return. Use this time to explore further strategies for coping with cravings and consider reaching out to support groups or resources designed to help individuals quit smoking.
